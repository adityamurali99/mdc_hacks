UMich Student Legal Services - Tips on Subletting & Fraud Warning

Before you decide to sublet, make sure that you understand the process and any legal ramifications.
- Read your lease – usually you will need your Landlord's written consent to sublease.
- Sign a sublease agreement with your subtenant and keep a copy! Make sure that you include the total amount of rent to be paid, how rent will be paid, beginning and ending dates of the sublease.
- Before the subtenant moves in, or receives the keys, collect the security deposit, and first rental payment.

!!! FRAUD WARNING !!!
The Ann Arbor Police Department has confirmed a number of scams that target students and landlords via email requests to lease property. 

SCAM RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR:
- "Sight-Unseen" Demands: Please be wary of prospective renters, landlords, and subtenants who contact you expressing an interest to rent a space sight-unseen, often claiming they are "out of the country" on a "missionary trip" or "work assignment."
- Overpayment Scams: Beware of individuals sending an overpayment for deposit/rent, then asking for the overpayment to be sent back to them. This is a scam because their check will not clear the bank.
- Untraceable Payment Methods: Legitimate landlords and subtenants will not demand a security deposit via wire transfer, Zelle, CashApp, or cryptocurrency before a verified tour.
- Post-Move-In Payment: Beware of sublet tenants asking to pay rent after they move in. Your student should always obtain payment before the subtenant moves in.
